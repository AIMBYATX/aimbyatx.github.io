Affordability in my Backyard

Affordability in my Backyard is an open collective of Austinites advocating for a CodeNEXT that will maximize affordability, combat gentrification and prevent displacement. We want a CodeNEXT that puts people over profit. Read about the background, our proposals and our research below. If you like what you read, sign the petition, display a sign and join the fight.

The City's CodeNEXT Intro

Background

Austin is facing an affordability crisis. Rents and property values are continuing to rise displacing whole communities and threatening the very culture that makes Austin Austin. CodeNEXT is an unprecedented oppurtunity to create a Land Development Code that takes active steps to prevent displacement, combat gentrification and ensure that all classes of people can afford to live in Austin. In a sentence, CodeNEXT is a rewrite of the Land Development Code, which determines what types of buildings can be built and where they can be built.

Our current code was written in 1984. The City of Austin passed a comprehensive plan called Imagine Austin in 2012 that decided how the city will grow and develop into the future. CodeNEXT is the city's rewrite of our current land development code to help fulfill the goals of affordability, environmental stewardship, and inclusiveness.

Our current code disproportionately benefits wealthy property investors at the expense of the rest of us. As we work on CodeNEXT we must recognize the discrimnatory nature of our previous codes and create a code that does not place the responsibility solely on the backs of working class communities and communities of color.

The affordability crisis is playing out in cities all across the US. We can use the research and techniques other cities are implementing to create a CodeNEXT that will preserve the city we all love.

Our Proposed Fixes

CodeNEXT Suggestions

Regional Increase in Housing

CodeNEXT should allow for additional housing throughout all of Austin. Affordability is an issue that must be tackled regionally.4

Transportation Oriented

CodeNEXT should both increase housing along high frequency transit corridors and increase housing in areas that will make high frequency service viable. Shorter commutes are critical to help people escape poverty.

Equal

Ensure that the zoning is spread equally among the cities various communities. CodeNEXT should create equitable levels of development in the wealthy communities as the working class communities.

Density Bonuses

Create density bonuses that can be used in all residential zoning categories. Affordable (subsidized) housing is twice as effective at preventing displacement as market rate housing.2

Environmental

Focus on brownfield and already developed areas instead of greenfield development. Provide height and FAR bonuses in leiu of impervious cover increases to provide flood protection.

Open

All data from the computer modeling Austin is currently conducting should be shared with the community. We need to know what the models are showing precisely not just the overview.

In-Unit ADUs

Remove all stipulations around the creation of in-unit ADUs and allow them to be created by right. This will preserve existing structures while providing more housing units. Especially valuable in keeping older residents who are empty nesters in their homes.

Density Bonuses Override F25

Allow density bonuses to override any pre-existing zoning requirements, especially in the central located neighborhoods. Affordability should be allowed by right in these high oppurtunity neighborhoods.
